I built your housing dashboard before you asked me to

I wanted to understand Wake County's housing crisis the way HACR sees it — through data. So I built a full Housing Intelligence Dashboard from scratch: a Python pipeline processing 135,000+ rows of HUD and Census-style data, with automated cleaning, validation, and transformation into dashboard-ready tables.

The Streamlit dashboard has 5 tabs — covering homelessness system performance, regional comparisons across all 20 NC systems, housing affordability by income level, shelter capacity and exit outcomes, and annual point-in-time counts.

Nobody asked me to do this. I just wanted to show I could do the work before day one.

I think like a policy analyst, not just a data person

Housing data isn't just rows and columns — it's families paying 67% of their income on rent. It's shelter systems running at 85%+ capacity with nowhere to overflow. It's a median rent that climbed 86% in a decade while wages didn't keep up.

The dashboard I built doesn't just track HUD metrics. It translates system performance data, point-in-time counts, and CHAS affordability numbers into a story that decision-makers can act on — where the gaps are, who's falling through, and what it's costing the county.
