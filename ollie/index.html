Why Ollie Excites Me

I have a dog. I know exactly the feeling of wanting only the best for him - and I know how quickly a subscription becomes a non-negotiable part of his routine. That's what makes Ollie's retention problem so interesting: dog parents don't churn casually. When they do, it signals something real - a life change, a trust gap, a missed moment in the member journey. Finding those signals and acting on them before they lead to cancellation is the kind of problem I genuinely love working on.

I've spent 2+ years building the exact analytical toolkit this role needs - cohort analyses on member data, A/B tests from design to go/no-go, LTV curves that separate high-value from at-risk segments, and Looker dashboards on Snowflake that give lifecycle, product, and marketing teams real self-serve visibility. I'm also comfortable with imperfect business questions - the kind where the ask is fuzzy, the data is messy, and the real job is figuring out what question to answer before writing a single line of SQL. That's where I thrive.

Why Me

1

I think in subscription lifetime value - not just monthly retention rates

Retention rate is a lagging indicator. The real question is: which members have high LTV potential, which are signaling early churn risk, and what does the lifecycle look like between acquisition and win-back? I've built analytics that answer all three.

At S2N Health, I built Looker dashboards surfacing cohort and retention metrics across user segments, cutting recurring ad-hoc requests by 50%. I conducted cohort analysis on member enrollment data using SQL and Python, surfacing retention and engagement patterns across 3+ segments - identifying which cohorts were trending toward churn weeks before it happened, giving the product team time to intervene.

At Capgemini, I spearheaded quantitative analysis identifying trends that drove $150K+ in revenue opportunities - specifically by separating high-LTV segments from at-risk ones and building the reporting layer that made that distinction visible to leadership. I also consolidated transactional datasets from 5+ sources into Snowflake, eliminating the 3-hour manual consolidation step that was blocking lifecycle analysis from happening in the first place.

Cohort LTV curves, early churn signals, reactivation windows - I build the full subscription analytics picture.

2

I run A/B tests end-to-end - and I know when the results are lying

Thoughtful test design for lifecycle comms and new member experiences isn't just about calculating a p-value. It's about knowing when the test was underpowered, when the cohort has a selection problem, and when "statistically significant" still doesn't mean you should ship it.

At Capgemini, I analyzed 15+ A/B tests across product features using SQL and Python, quantifying lift across user segments and informing go/no-go decisions that shipped 5+ product updates. I owned the full experimental workflow - from hypothesis framing and sample size calculation, through measurement, to the final recommendation. I flagged when results were misleading and helped teams avoid making product decisions on noise.

I also built Prophet forecasting models on historical data in Python, reducing forecast error from 12% MAPE to 9% - the kind of iterative, hypothesis-driven quantitative work that makes the entire experimentation program sharper over time.

From test design to the go/no-go decision - I own the whole experimental loop, including knowing when to pump the brakes.

3

I thrive on imperfect questions - the fuzzier the ask, the better

The most valuable retention insights don't come from a clean, well-scoped analysis request. They come from someone sitting in a cross-functional meeting, listening to a half-formed product question, and knowing how to break it down into something answerable. That's my favorite part of the job.

At Capgemini, I partnered with product, engineering, and marketing teams to turn ambiguous business questions into measurable analyses, shipping 10+ cross-functional deliverables - each starting from a fuzzy "why are users doing X?" and ending with a clear recommendation. I spearheaded quantitative analysis across 10+ business units, surfacing $150K+ in revenue opportunities by asking the question behind the question.

At S2N Health, I partnered with engineering on reporting specs across NoSQL and relational sources - aligning 8+ report specifications across 3 data sources. I also automated daily production data monitoring using Python, reclaiming 8+ analyst hours per week previously lost to manual checks - freeing that time for the exploratory work that actually creates new hypotheses.

I don't wait for a clean question. I help the team figure out what question is actually worth answering.
